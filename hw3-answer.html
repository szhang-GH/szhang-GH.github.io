    1. In the class we learn that for a matrix \mathbf{A}=\begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}, \mathbf{A}^{-1} = \frac{1}{ad - bc} \begin{bmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{bmatrix}. Use this formula to compute (\mathbf{X}'\mathbf{X})^{-1} by hand. Further compute by hand the estimated regression coefficients using the matrix formula \mathbf{b} = (\mathbf{X}'\mathbf{X})^{-1}\mathbf{X}'\mathbf{Y}.

    Answer:

    From the output in (a), we have: \mathbf{X}'\mathbf{X} = \begin{bmatrix} 50 & 770 \\ 770 & 13228 \end{bmatrix}

    The determinant is: det(\mathbf{X}'\mathbf{X}) = ad - bc = 50(13228) - 770(770) = 661400 - 592900 = 68500

    The inverse is: (\mathbf{X}'\mathbf{X})^{-1} = \frac{1}{68500} \begin{bmatrix} 13228 & -770 \\ -770 & 50 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{13228}{68500} & \frac{-770}{68500} \\ \frac{-770}{68500} & \frac{50}{68500} \end{bmatrix} \approx \begin{bmatrix} 0.193109 & -0.011241 \\ -0.011241 & 0.000730 \end{bmatrix}

    We also have \mathbf{X}'\mathbf{Y} = \begin{bmatrix} 2149 \\ 38482 \end{bmatrix}.

    The estimated regression coefficients \mathbf{b} are: \mathbf{b} = (\mathbf{X}'\mathbf{X})^{-1}\mathbf{X}'\mathbf{Y} \approx \begin{bmatrix} 0.193109 & -0.011241 \\ -0.011241 & 0.000730 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 2149 \\ 38482 \end{bmatrix}

    = \begin{bmatrix} 0.193109(2149) + (-0.011241)(38482) \\ (-0.011241)(2149) + 0.000730(38482) \end{bmatrix}

    \approx \begin{bmatrix} 414.9912 - 432.5762 \\ -24.1569 + 28.0919 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -17.585 \\ 3.935 \end{bmatrix}

    The rank of \mathbf{H} equals its trace, which is 2 in this case.

    Explanation: The trace of the hat matrix is the sum of its diagonal elements. For a hat matrix in linear regression, the trace is equal to the rank of the matrix, which corresponds to the number of parameters p in the model (rank of \mathbf{X}). Here, tr(\mathbf{H}) = 2, which matches the number of regression coefficients (\beta_0 and \beta_1).

    Explanation: Observe that \mathbf{X}_c'\mathbf{X}_c is a diagonal matrix because the off-diagonal elements are is the inner project of \mathbf{1} and the centered predictor speed_c, which equals 0 because (\sum (x_i - \bar{x}) = 0).

    The fitted values \hat{\mathbf{Y}}_c are identical to \hat{\mathbf{Y}} from (f). This is because the column space spanned by the centered design matrix \mathbf{X}_c = [\mathbf{1}, \mathbf{x} - \bar{x}\mathbf{1}] is identical to the column space spanned by the original design matrix \mathbf{X} = [\mathbf{1}, \mathbf{x}]. To see this, any linear combination of the columns of \mathbf{X}, a \mathbf{1} + b \mathbf{x}, can be rewritten as a linear combination of the columns of \mathbf{X}_c, a' \mathbf{1} + b' (\mathbf{x} - \bar{x}\mathbf{1}), where a' = a + b\bar{x} and b' = b. Thus, the column spaces are the same.

    The hat matrix \mathbf{H} (and \mathbf{H}_c) represents the projection operator onto this column space. Since the subspace is the same, the projection of \mathbf{Y} onto it yields the same fitted values.
